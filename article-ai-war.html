Winning the Consumer AI Promotion War

Key Arguments

In the early stages of China’s LLM development, firms competed heavily on technical benchmarks: parameter size, reasoning capabilities and training efficiency. Baidu, an early pioneer, positioned its Ernie model as a technologically sophisticated solution for both enterprise and public use. As the market matured, however, the focus of competition shifted dramatically. Consumers demonstrated little interest in abstract technical specifications, instead gravitating towards tools that felt intuitive, conversational and aligned with their daily routines. What emerged was a race not for superior models, but for superior entry points — the digital portals through which users first and most regularly interact with AI. This reorientation allowed late arrivals, like Doubao, to outpace early technical leaders by centring user experience over theoretical performance.

Doubao’s most defining advantage comes from its seamless integration within ByteDance’s ecosystem, most notably Douyin and Toutiao, two of China’s largest content and social media platforms. This infrastructure provides steady user traffic and continuous feedback that strengthens the model over time. Equally important, however, is the distinctive data foundation that separates Doubao from its rivals. Unlike competing platforms, Doubao is trained on vast volumes of contemporary social content: short-video scripts, viral trends, internet slang, entertainment discussions and informal dialogue drawn directly from Douyin and Toutiao. This grounding in everyday digital culture allows Doubao to understand and mirror the tone, humour and references familiar to average Chinese internet users. By contrast, Baidu’s Ernie draws from search results, encyclopaedic databases and formal web text, resulting in accurate but often stiff and impersonal responses. Alibaba’s Qwen prioritises e-commerce content and productivity materials, making it practical for shopping and work but less suited for casual interaction. Because Doubao’s knowledge base reflects the lived language and culture of its users, it carries a natural relatability that has become central to its mainstream appeal.

The stakes of this consumer AI promotion war have become increasingly visible across major platforms. Alibaba’s Qwen took a highly direct approach to mass user acquisition, launching a high-profile campaign offering free milk tea to anyone who downloaded and used the app. Users simply needed to type or speak a request for the AI to order a drink, and they received vouchers covering the full cost at major chain stores across the country. The campaign generated enormous public attention and drove a sharp surge in downloads, reflecting the lengths to which rival platforms are prepared to go to challenge Doubao’s lead. Tencent’s Yuanbao similarly expanded its reach through substantial incentives, including high-profile red envelope campaigns tied to WeChat Pay that quickly elevated its daily active user base from millions to hundreds of millions. Baidu also introduced aggressive promotional measures to boost adoption of Ernie. Meanwhile, ByteDance avoided reliance on financial subsidies and instead deepened Doubao’s organic integration into daily content consumption and social interaction. This contrast shows a key difference between Doubao and its competitors: while rivals entered the promotion war to attract users through rewards, Doubao solidified its position by becoming part of how users naturally communicate, create and explore online.

Doubao further distinguishes itself from its competitors through exclusive creative tools that enhance its everyday usability. ByteDance has integrated its advanced Seedance AI video generation model directly into Doubao, allowing users to generate original video content from simple text prompts. This multimedia functionality sets Doubao apart as a creative, all-in-one assistant, rather than a purely text-based tool — a feature not offered by Qwen, Ernie or the Yuanbao app in a similarly accessible, consumer-friendly format. This focus on creative expression reinforces Doubao’s connection to young, digitally engaged users and strengthens its position as the most versatile and appealing option in the market.

Beyond visual and creative tools, Doubao also leads its rivals in highly natural, human-like AI voice synthesis. Its voice system delivers expressive, conversational, and emotionally responsive audio output that closely mimics real human speech, whereas competing assistants often sound robotic, flat, or overly formal. Doubao offers over 100+ voices, covering multilingual, emotional, role-play, and professional tones; there are even Disney character voices. It also supports four Chinese dialects (Cantonese, Sichuanese, Northeast, and Shaanxi). This superior voice technology further enhances user comfort and engagement, reinforcing Doubao’s identity as a relatable, user-centred daily tool rather than a purely functional or technical device.
